NACA 1135

Used daily in AERO 303 here at A&M this document now dates over half a century old and I believe this helps to show the strength of the formuluations used and provides a look into why this class is so useful. Especially in todays aerospace industries focus on supersonic and hypersonic flight. To use enter a single known value and the closest value to the fourth signifcant figure will be chosen and all other values returned.

Below are the gereral decriptions of the different NACA 1135 columns.

  • M1   -  local mach number/upstream mach number
  • p/pt   -  static to total pressure ratio
  • ρ/ρt   -  static to total density ratio
  • T/Tt   -  static to total temperature ratio
  • β   -   the square root of M squared-1, NOT related to δ-β-mach
  • q/pt   -   ratio of dynamic pressure to total pressure
  • A/A*   -   ratio of local cross-sectional area to choked flow cross sectional area
  • V/a*   -   local speed to speed of sound ratio
  • ν   -   Prandtl-Meyer angle, given in degrees
  • μ   -   mach angle
  • M2   -   mach number downstream of NORMAL shock, (across oblique shocks use M1sin(β))
  • p2/p1   -   ratio of static pressures across normal shock
  • ρ   -   ratio of static density across normal shock
  • T2/T1   -   ratio of static temperature across normal shock
  • pt2/pt1   -   total pressure ratio across normal shock
  • p1/pt2   -   ratio of static pressure upstream to total downstream pressure
