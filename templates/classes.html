Classes

As of now I have completed 114 credit hours in my college career and I always wanted to have a little insight into what I would be getting into before the start of every semester. My goal is to do that for others with classes I have taken in the past as well as pass on some of the notes that I have made. Below I will list the classes I find need these such things and as such not all 114 credit hours will be covered. That would be insane. You can hover over each class to see a brief description and click to see a more detailed description with attached notes. (Some classes have more notes than others.)



I have also listed the semester I took it and what grade I recieved.

Engr 102

Spring Semester 2019 - Engineering Lab I Computation
Grade Recieved: A
ENGR 102


Teaches basics of engineering computation. Emphasis is based on general programming knowledge as in top-down versus bottom-up design. A goal in placed on mastering if statements as well as for/while loops with a brief introduction to function based programming.

Math 151

Spring Semester 2020 - Engineering Math I
Grade Recieved: B
MATH 151


Introduces the fundamentals of calculus which could be considered along with physics 1, the basis of your engineering degree. Leaving this course you should have a good grasp of derivatives ,(not partials yet), as well as limits, L'Hôpital's rule, and basic single integrals.
